# Idea intake: semantic-policy headroom tournament

## Direction

Insert a no-training qualification stage between failure forensics and
capability production. Measure the exact learned parent on multiple semantic
conflicts under the intended failed-test state, then mechanically admit only
replicated non-saturated axes to later curriculum work.

## Closest prior

`qwen35_4b_validation_policy_counterexample_curriculum` is the direct
predecessor. Its candidate was local, but explicit rule text plus an otherwise-
correct partial made parent and matched control perfect before candidate
exposure. `qwen35_4b_specialist_policy_integration` independently established
the analogous per-teacher headroom rule. This experiment applies that lesson
to procedural curriculum substrates.

## Non-duplication

No prior repository experiment crosses explicit versus verifier-inferred
semantic contracts, multiple exception/rejection conflicts, and multiple data
representations solely to qualify parent headroom before training. It is not a
new SFT dose, policy selector, sampling baseline, or benchmark evaluation.

## Expected information

- One or more eligible axes: a defensible, replicated substrate exists for the
  next counterexample curriculum.
- Explicit controls pass but inferred axes saturate: the parent can infer all
  tested conflicts; retire this curriculum direction or design genuinely
  deeper semantics.
- Explicit controls pass but inferred axes are near zero: the task may be too
  ambiguous/unlearnable; do not train without a separate learnability probe.
- Explicit controls fail: harness/specification fidelity is inadequate; the
  qualification instrument itself fails.
- Shape disagreement: representation, not semantic axis, controls headroom;
  future training must stratify or narrow accordingly.

## Contamination boundary

Fresh procedural repositories only. Hidden checks and repair patches stay
host-side. Menagerie is forbidden, and no benchmark source, item, transcript,
or result is read.

## Decision

Proceed with two fixed blocks, one fixed parent, explicit controls, and frozen
eligibility rules. Preserve all axes regardless of outcome.
