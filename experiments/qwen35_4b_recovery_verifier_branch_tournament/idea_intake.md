# Idea intake: public-verifier recovery branch tournament

## Direction

Use visible executable evidence to choose between the two locality-safe recovery
policies whose hidden-success union replicated at 78.75%, then use a positive
tournament only as the capability producer for a later balanced curriculum.

## Closest prior work

- `qwen35_4b_recovery_payload_budget_harness` is the direct predecessor. It
  fixed payload/transition measurement, passed dev, and stopped when λ=.18 tied
  action-only on confirmation; its paired audit supplies the complementary
  source policies.
- `qwen35_4b_same_prefix_advantage_routing` tried to predict a local teacher
  from sampled advantage and found statewise winner labels unstable. This
  successor does not predict: it executes both bounded branches and uses a
  public test result.
- `qwen35_4b_real_sample_verify_commit` and C46 establish the ordering principle
  that visible execution evidence precedes confidence or self-judgment.

## Non-duplication

No prior recovery experiment prospectively combines two frozen local policies
with a public verifier under a same-policy, equal-reservation pass-if-either
baseline. This is not another interpolation dose, family router, DAgger pass,
or hidden-oracle selector.

## Expected information

- Positive: the source-policy complementarity is deployably capturable and can
  generate better-than-sampling winner traces for curriculum compression.
- Negative: the 78.75% union is mostly public-evidence-inseparable or fails on
  new algorithms; do not spend on winner banking.

## Decision

Proceed with a frozen selector, four new procedural families, two prospective
blocks, feasibility before selector scoring, and no Menagerie in this
harness-only experiment.
