# Adversarial design review (workflow)

**Verdict:** sound_with_fixes

## Confounds
- FINITE-DSL MEMORIZATION (make-or-break): the depth-3 solution space over a fixed 16-op DSL is finite/enumerable. As N grows (40->400 and the proposed harvest), you mechanically enumerate a larger fraction of that space, so the probability that a held-out task's solution SKELETON (op-composition) already sits in training rises with N by construction. A monotone-rising curve is then the expected signature of memorization, not of elicited representational capacity. Must measure and hold constant 'fraction of eval solution-skeletons present in train' across doses.
- SIGNATURE-ONLY DEDUP LEAKS THE SOLUTION: 'behavioral function-signature dedup' dedups on I/O signature, but two different signatures can share the identical depth-3 op-composition/solution program. So the held-out set can contain tasks whose SOLUTION is literally in training. Dedup must be on the solution skeleton, not the signature.
- BRUTE-SEARCH EASY-BIAS: interpreter brute search finds the search-cheap (shorter / fewer-branch) depth-3 programs first; the harvestable 400 are the easy tail. A random depth-3 held-out set includes harder programs the training never demonstrates -> a built-in ceiling independent of N that reads as a 'representational cap.' Report the search-budget/program-length distribution of the 400 vs the eval set.
- MIXTURE DRIFT OF THE DEPTH-1+2 SCAFFOLD: at fixed epochs the depth-3 share of data goes ~24% (170 pairs) -> ~75% (530 pairs); the decomposition scaffold the depth-3 skill rides on can shift with dose. Report depth-2 held-out coverage per arm as a guardrail covariate.
- CHECKPOINT/SCHEDULE ARTIFACT: epochs=3 was tuned for C22, not for each N. The generalization optimum likely differs by N; a rise could be 'N=400 happened to land near a better checkpoint.' Sweep checkpoints / early-stop on a validation coverage so each dose gets its best shot.
- METRIC MULTIPLICITY: three metrics (think cov@16, no-think greedy@1, no-think cov@16) x monotone tests. Reporting 'a rise in any of them' inflates false-positive rate. Pre-register one primary.
- RENDERING STEREOTYPY: 400 brute solutions rendered to a canonical DSL code style can teach surface skeleton; no-think greedy may regurgitate one stereotyped skeleton that only passes matching tasks. Vary rendering / verify semantic diversity.
- SINGLE-REALIZATION DRAW: one nested draw and one training seed per dose means the curve is a single realization; a lone unusually (un)helpful example or data-order roll can create a spurious slope.

## Training-steps issue
"Yes, at fixed epochs=3 the dose confounds three things at once: (a) more distinct depth-3 examples, (b) more total optimizer steps (170*3 vs 530*3 example-passes), and (c) a shifting depth-3:depth-1+2 mixture ratio (~24% -> ~75%). Physically, 'more depth-3 data' and 'more depth-3 gradient exposure' are the SAME event and cannot be separated -- do not pretend otherwise. But 'more depth-3' vs 'more total training' IS separable and must be separated. Honest handling: (1) match total optimizer steps across all arms (train to a fixed step budget, not fixed epochs) so the small-N arms just repeat their examples more -- this removes the total-compute confound; (2) hold the number of passes over the depth-1+2 subset constant across arms so the depth-2 scaffold doesn't drift (report depth-2 coverage as a guardrail); (3) add a decisive control arm: 40 DISTINCT depth-3 pairs upsampled to a 400 count, trained at the same step budget and mixture as the N=400 arm. If upsampled-40 ~= distinct-400, the gain is gradient/mixture exposure, not data diversity (so 'data-limited' in the interesting sense is false); if distinct-400 > upsampled-40, that gap is the genuine distinct-data effect. Frame the dose axis explicitly as '# distinct depth-3 examples at matched compute and matched depth-3 token budget,' and report the curve both at fixed-epochs and fixed-steps so the reader sees whether the trend survives compute-matching."

## Nesting
"Make INDEPENDENT random draws per dose the primary design, with >=3 training seeds per dose (each seed = a fresh size-N subset + different data order + different LoRA init). A single nested chain (40 subset 130 subset 400) is one realization: it confounds the dose with the identity of which specific examples were added, so a single anomalously helpful/harmful example present in the 40 propagates into all arms, and you cannot put error bars on the dose effect. Given the huge binomial noise at these success counts, seed replication is what separates 'dose' from 'draw+training noise' -- without it the curve is uninterpretable. Keep the nested subsets ONLY as a secondary monotone-containment sanity view (it guarantees the low doses are literal subsets), not as the evidentiary backbone. Net: independent draws x >=3 seeds for the inferential claim; nested as a supporting illustration."

## Power
"n_eval=40 is badly underpowered for a 3-point trend. At p~0.1-0.2 the per-point binomial SE is ~0.05-0.07, so two doses whose true rates differ by <~0.15 are indistinguishable, and a 3-point curve barely defines monotonicity. The deployable no-think greedy@1 at ~0.00-0.05 is a rare-event detector: to detect a rise from ~0.02 to ~0.10 at 80% power needs hundreds of tasks per arm. Concrete fixes: (1) n_eval >= ~150 depth-3 held-out tasks (procedural substrate, cheap), on ONE frozen paired set shared across base+all doses; (2) PRIMARY metric = think coverage@16, analyzed with a mixed-effects logistic regression solve ~ log(N) + (1|task) + (1|seed), reporting the log-N slope and its CI -- not independent chi-squares (the paired frozen set demands a paired/mixed model, e.g. Cochran-Armitage on paired outcomes as a check); (3) for the deployable read do NOT headline greedy@1 -- use the per-sample solve rate over the 16 coverage samples (16*n_eval Bernoulli trials) or no-think pass@16 as the dense metric, and report greedy@1 only as confirmatory once the dense metric clears zero; (4) use >=4 LOG-SPACED doses extending past 400 (e.g., 40/80/160/320/640) so a plateau can actually be observed -- 3 points cannot distinguish a saturating rise from a plateau; (5) pre-register the decision rule: 'DATA-LIMITED' = log-N slope CI excludes 0 and is positive AND the effect survives the memorization control (eval-skeleton-in-train held constant); 'REPRESENTATIONAL CAP' = slope CI includes 0 AND the top-dose CI overlaps the low-dose CI (state it as an equivalence/CI-overlap conclusion, never a bare non-significant p)."

## Must-fix
- Measure and hold constant across doses the fraction of held-out eval tasks whose solution SKELETON (op-composition) is present in the training subset. On a finite DSL, more N mechanically raises this fraction, so an uncontrolled rising curve is expected from memorization and says nothing about representational capacity. This is the make-or-break control.
- Dedup train vs eval on the solution op-composition/program skeleton, not just the behavioral function signature -- otherwise the exact thing being measured leaks into training.
- Raise n_eval to >=~150 depth-3 held-out on one frozen paired set; replace deployable greedy@1 as the headline with a dense metric (per-sample solve rate over the 16 samples, or no-think pass@16); keep greedy@1 confirmatory only.
- Run >=3 independent training seeds per dose (fresh size-N subset + data order + LoRA init); report error bars. Demote the single nested chain to a secondary containment view.
- Deconfound data-diversity from gradient exposure: match total optimizer steps across arms (fixed-step, not fixed-epoch) and add the upsampled-40-to-400 control at matched steps/mixture; hold depth-1+2 pass-count constant and report depth-2 coverage as a guardrail. Report the curve at both fixed-epochs and fixed-steps.
- Use >=4 log-spaced doses extending past 400 (e.g., 40/80/160/320/640) so a plateau is observable, and pre-register the mixed-effects log-N trend test plus the explicit data-limited-vs-cap decision rule (including an equivalence/CI-overlap criterion for 'cap', not a bare n.s.).
- Characterize the structural and search-difficulty (program-length / search-budget-to-find) distribution of the harvested 400 vs the eval set; if the 400 are the search-easy tail, a plateau is a coverage/easy-bias artifact, not a representational cap, and must be flagged as such.
- Pre-register a single primary metric (think coverage@16) and correct for the multiplicity of the three reported metrics; do not report 'a rise in any metric' as a dose-response.
