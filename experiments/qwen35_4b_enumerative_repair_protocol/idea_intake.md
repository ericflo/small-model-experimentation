# Idea Intake: Enumerative-Repair Protocol

## Trigger

Owner steer: the closures covered the tested classes, not the idea
space. Re-reading the map: every failed menders attack taught the model
to INFER the right repair — asserted examples, demonstrated search,
episode feedback, 800-row scale, and even 2AFC verification all sit at
walls or chance. Nothing ever taught the opposite of cleverness:
SYSTEMATIC ENUMERATION. Meanwhile the corpus's own laws point exactly
there — C34 (brute-force search dominates the model's reasoning,
everywhere measured, a model-level law) and the protocol-install law
(hygiene, explore, termination, statechain: turn-by-turn protocols are
the ONE thing this model reliably learns). The menders family is a
bounded multi-turn episode WITH rerun feedback: the environment itself
verifies, so an enumerator converts turn budget into coverage without
touching the walled inference skill.

## Question

Does a 160-row single-kind dose (full concentration per the freshly
hardened dilution rule) teach the enumeration discipline — name the next
untried legal candidate in a documented canonical order — and does that
discipline convert to the menders family at a sealed medium event?

## Honest calibration

- Install: the skill is mechanical (enumerate, cross off, emit next) and
  protocols are 4-for-4 in this line — ~60–70% for local promotion.
- Menders conversion: the transfer bet is that the family's episodes
  reward coverage within their turn budget, which public metadata calls
  "bounded" without saying how tightly — ~20–30%. The enumeration-
  fidelity decomposition (legal? untried? canonical-next? per holdout
  row) makes even a miss mechanically legible.
- The conversion law's lineage caveat applies: this dose BUILDS its own
  substrate on the clean parent rather than assuming it.

## Why the kill rules do not bind

The closed rules cover repair-content pedagogy (asserted, demonstrated,
episode-feedback) and dose scale of the SAME inference lesson. This
teaches a different behavior entirely — the one C34 says wins.
