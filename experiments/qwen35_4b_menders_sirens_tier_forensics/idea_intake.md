# Idea Intake: Menders/Sirens + Tier Forensics

## Trigger

The goal-gap pilot closed with "menders = 0 and sirens = 0.500 for EVERY arm
at EVERY seed at quick/tb1024 — the goal's remaining wall is two families
wide," and the backlog queued forensics on those two constants as the
prerequisite before any new treatment. The repository already holds every
gateway receipt the program has ever produced, including a large medium-tier
corpus from the earlier gym line — enough to adjudicate the constants and
the goal-gate venue with zero GPU.

## Question

Are the two constants structural walls of the model, or artifacts of the
quick instrument's granularity and item draws — and at which tier is the
goal gate (candidate strictly above base on all ten public families)
actually passable?

## Why believable

Pure receipt analysis: the answer already exists in committed artifacts.
No model event, no seed, no benchmark content — `benchmarks/` is never read;
inputs are gateway OUTPUT receipts under `experiments/*/runs/`.

## Calibration

High probability of a decisive read (the data is complete and in-repo);
the only risk is receipt-schema heterogeneity, handled by frozen cleaning
rules with the raw table preserved.
