# Idea Intake: Medium Intermediate Budget Probe

## Trigger

The tb8192 probe closed BUDGET_GATE_STOP at its first arm: the gateway's
hard per-arm wall budget refused base before any treated arm ran. The
thinking-budget lever — the last cheap non-training lever on the binding
menders constraint — survives only at intermediate settings. This is its
last believable test.

## Question

At medium/tb4096 on a fresh sealed seed, does the event even fit the
gateway's wall budget for all four arms — and if it does, does menders
(zero for every arm at tb1024) or rites (zero for three of four) move
with 4× thinking room?

## Calibration

Feasibility is a genuine coin flip: base ran medium/tb1024 in 157 s and
failed at tb8192; hygiene_explore was the slowest arm (230 s) and may
bind first at 4,096. The frozen stop contract prices a failure at one
arm's compute. If the event completes, a menders movement re-opens the
10-family ceiling (worth ~25% given C44's serial-compute law and the
truncation-cascade history); a rites unlock for hygiene_explore alone
would put that arm at 9/10 with no new dose. A second stop closes the
lever entirely and fixes the statechain successor's 9/10 ceiling as the
program's honest position — also decisive.
