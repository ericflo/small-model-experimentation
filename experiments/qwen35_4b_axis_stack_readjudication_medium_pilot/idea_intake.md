## Gate-shopping risk, addressed directly

Re-judging artifacts that failed a gate is p-hacking when the bar moves after
the data and the old failure is erased. Here: (a) both prior failures remain
recorded and their seeds remain sealed — nothing is erased; (b) the correction
was queued in the program backlog, with its exact mechanism, BEFORE this
experiment opened, from evidence in two independent receipts; (c) the new gate
uses a fresh task seed, so no graded item is reused; (d) the corrected bar is
HARDER in one direction (a fail-closed GATE_UNDETECTABLE outcome exists) and
merely stops one control's ceiling from silently tightening the quota; (e) the
adversarial design review is instructed to scrutinize exactly this risk.

## Mechanism

Unchanged from the stack trial: the question is whether the already-installed

whether medium-tier granularity converts them. The explanation is false if the
candidate fails the corrected gate on fresh tasks, or passes locally and shows
nothing at medium.
