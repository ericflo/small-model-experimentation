## Novelty Claim

This is the first universal-curriculum trial to condition supervised corrective
continuations on the authenticated parent's own fresh procedural failure prefixes
rather than on idealized complete traces.

## Mechanism

Fresh procedural tasks elicit the exact deployment states the parent actually visits.
An executable oracle identifies the first observable failure and supplies only the
minimal continuation needed to recover: close and serialize a correct state, reject a
reference declaration as an operation, terminate a repeated induction search with a
verified hypothesis, recompute probe counts, or propagate a repaired state. Masking
the observed prefix from loss should teach the correction without imitating or
rewriting the entire trajectory. The explanation is false if the candidate does not
strictly beat both parent and exact-token replay overall and on execute/induct/probe.

## Control Plan

- Baseline: authenticated `close_xi` parent.
- Mechanism-falsifying control: independent same-parent replay continuation with
  identical encoded forward tokens, optimizer steps, backend, seed, and aligned
  shared replay positions.
- Additional safeguards: fresh task IDs and seeds; byte-level disjointness from all
  prior local gates; bounded failure-class quotas; prefix/provenance receipts; exact
  target recomputation; no selection using a held-out outcome.
- Hidden-label boundary: no `benchmarks/` read or import. Aggregate-only evaluation is
  conditional on local promotion and cannot provide training data.
