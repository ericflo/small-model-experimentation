# Adversarial design review

Self-review was used because repository policy forbids delegation for this run. It occurred before result-bearing generation, training, or evaluation.

## Verdict

**Proceed with safeguards.** Conditional recovery banking directly targets the mechanism C54 deleted. It is worth the GPU spend only with a happy-context mechanism control, an external scaffold, matched sampling, family-held-out transfer, normal-loop retention, and locality.

## Attacks and dispositions

1. **This repeats C54 with more labels.** Global operator balance cannot preserve a conditional policy. **Fix:** construct actual public rejected-patch and failed-test states, balance loss at seven state→action strata, and report transition behavior directly.
2. **The host oracle teaches solutions.** A partial repair or oracle could leak into rows. **Fix:** final targets come only from successful model trajectories. The host partial creates an unresolved state; the oracle is restricted to fixture tests/smoke. Firewall checks reject oracle/partial fields and hidden executables.
3. **Success selection removes failures again.** The selected trajectory may contain no useful recovery. **Fix:** use its execution-verified final patch as the endpoint, then replay that endpoint from deterministic failure states. The conditioning state, not accidental trajectory wandering, supplies the recovery lesson.
4. **Happy and recovery arms have different code-token lengths.** Exact action bytes cannot remain executable in both initial and partially edited workspaces. **Fix:** match tasks, seven rows/task, operators, transition labels, optimizer exposure, and exact weighted action mass at every stratum. The decisive think-plan comparison remains byte-identical action versus reason rows.
5. **The plan text is decorative.** **Fix:** select between recovery action-only and byte-identical 5%-plan arms on a train-family calibration block; transfer sees only one frozen candidate.
6. **A prompt can do the same thing.** **Fix:** require the trained candidate to beat the frozen base with an explicit post-failure recovery rule. If not, training is unjustified even if it beats unscaffolded base.
7. **Serial depth merely spends more inference.** **Fix:** compare one six-turn recovery loop with two independently sampled three-turn loops at the same call and maximum-token reservation.
8. **Training from C54 hides the source of gains.** **Fix:** every arm and the base share the exact immutable C54 apex checkpoint. The incremental recovery delta is the only trained difference; lineage hashes are recorded.
9. **Train-family arm selection contaminates the claim.** **Fix:** selection uses only new train-family tasks and a frozen lexicographic rule. Four algorithmically different families are untouched until the candidate is fixed, then repeated at new seeds.
10. **The injected state is artificial.** Controlled starts isolate a recovery policy and avoid depending on random failures, but could overstate end-to-end utility. **Fix:** also require normal-start retention and use Menagerie only after transfer. The claim is verifier-conditioned recovery, not production SWE competence.
11. **Balancing rare COMMIT tokens causes shared-weight damage.** **Fix:** low LR, short warm-start training, a 0.15 unrelated-logit ceiling, verify/commit behavioral gates, and immediate cancellation on locality failure.
12. **Entropy becomes an after-the-fact optimizer.** **Fix:** entropy/varentropy is preregistered as descriptive only; it cannot alter rows, weights, selection, gates, or benchmark access.
13. **Hidden tests become iterative feedback.** **Fix:** hidden output is never model-visible and banking receives only final pass booleans. There is no hidden-failure repair loop.
14. **Menagerie becomes a tuning set.** **Fix:** no seeds are assigned until two white-box transfer blocks pass. One candidate gets two paired aggregate-only events; any prerequisite failure leaves the suite untouched.
15. **A claim collision is likely.** **Fix:** reserve no claim number before the result. Update the ledger only after rebasing main and assigning the next available ID.

## Residual risks accepted

- Small procedural repositories are a mechanistic substrate, not a production coding benchmark. Family-held-out transfer and Menagerie bound the conclusion.
- Four transfer families and 80 controlled cases/block may miss small effects. The gates intentionally demand a large, repeatable improvement because the north star is capability elicitation, not a detectable policy twitch.
- Teacher-forced entropy at a registered seam is not identical to free-running uncertainty after a generated thought. It remains diagnostic only.
