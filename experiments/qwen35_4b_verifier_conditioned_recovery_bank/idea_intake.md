## Novelty Claim

The intervention unit is the verifier-conditioned **state→action transition**, not the global tool operator or an isolated think token. Successful model repairs are replayed into deterministic rejected-patch and failed-test states, and their loss mass is balanced at seven transition strata while preserving VERIFY and COMMIT.

## Related Claims

- C5: simple state rules can dominate trained tool policies.
- C50: causal action supervision at the emission seam can transfer.
- C52: shared-weight locality is a necessary gate for think-channel steering.
- C54: success-only operator compression deletes recovery behavior despite exact operator marginals.

## Mechanism

Qwen3.5-4B already often knows a correct repair, but after executable rejection it follows a brittle continuation policy: repeat an attractor patch, wander, or commit too early. Conditioning action supervision on the actual public failure state should install a recovery policy without demanding a new algorithmic solution. The explanation is false if the matched happy-path arm changes behavior equally, a one-line external scaffold matches the trained policy, family-held-out repositories do not improve, or unrelated logits drift past the registered ceiling.

## Control Plan

- Shift checks: six train algorithm families, four wholly different transfer families, a new-seed confirmation block, normal-start retention, and unrelated-context logit locality.
- Hidden-label boundary: visible failures enter conversations; private executables are host-only and become only booleans in receipts. No benchmark content is read or trained on.
