# Seed Prototypes

The imported tracks are valuable because they demonstrate how this repository should work, not because they define all future work.

## What They Prototyped

- Self-contained experiment folders with local code, data, runs, analysis, and reports.
- Fast iteration from smoke runs to larger pilots.
- Controls such as random, shuffled, corrupted, frozen, and oracle ceilings.
- Reports that separate mechanism, result, caveat, and next direction.
- The need for shared synthesis so future work does not repeat solved or falsified ideas.

## What They Should Not Do

- They should not limit the repository to Qwen-only work.
- They should not limit future work to the same two substrates.
- They should not force every new idea into the same mechanism family.
- They should not make source-track provenance more important than research-program direction.

## How Future Work Should Use Them

Use them as:

- seed evidence for current programs,
- examples of artifact layout,
- examples of controls and reports,
- cautionary evidence about selection, memory, and weak public tests.

Then branch beyond them.
